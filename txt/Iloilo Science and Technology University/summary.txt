





EXECUTIVE SUMMARY



A)	Introduction



The Iloilo Science and Technology University (ISAT-U), formerly known as Western Visayas College of Science and Technology (WVCST) and Iloilo School of Arts and Trades (ISAT), was initially established in 1905 as the Iloilo Trade School. On May 18, 1983, the school was converted into a state college by virtue of Batas Pambansa Blg. 395. 



On June 4, 2013, Republic Act No. 10595 was enacted converting the Western Visayas College of Science and Technology into a State University, to be known as the Iloilo Science and Technology University.  However, the establishment or conversion of the WVCST into a state university was only effected on May 18, 2015 after complying with the requirements for a university status, as determined by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED).



The University envisions on becoming a leading science and technology university in Southeast Asia by 2030, committed to provide quality and relevant advanced education, higher technological, professional instruction and training in arts, sciences, education, architecture, engineering, agriculture, forestry, and other fields of study, thereby producing locally oriented, globally competitive and eco-friendly human resources. It shall promote research and development programs to advance science and technology and undertake sustainable extension and production activities.



ICARE is the acronym for the University’s Vision-Mission which means: I-integrity, C-commitment, A-accountability, R-responsiveness and E-excellence.



The University has a main campus and four external campuses, comprising of Barotac Nuevo Campus, Dumangas Campus, Miag-ao Campus and the Leon Campus.



The Board of Regents is the governing board and the policy-making body of the university, which reviews and recommends to the University President, policies governing the administration, management, development and instructional planning of the university.



The University President recommends the policies to the Board of Regents for appropriate action and disseminates approved policies to the Department Heads, Faculty members and Administrative Staff.

D)	Auditor’s Opinion on the Financial Statements



The Auditor rendered a qualified opinion on the fairness of presentation of the financial statements of Iloilo Science and Technology University for the year ended December 31, 2020, due to doubtful existence and reliability of Property, Plant and Equipment (PPE) and Inventories totaling P10.797 million and P5.079 million, respectively, as stated in the Independent Auditor’s Report and as discussed in Part II of this Annual Audit Report.

















E)	Observations and Recommendations 



The following are the significant observations and the corresponding recommendations:



1)	Payments for the construction/repairs of Buildings and Other Structures totaling P81.500 million were supported with incomplete documentations contrary to Sec. 4(6) of Presidential Decree (PD) No. 1445, thus, propriety and validity of disbursements were not ensured. 

		

We recommended and the Management agreed to:



	a)		Direct the Accountant and the Planning Director to ensure completeness of information on supporting documents before payment to Contractors in accordance with Section 4.6 of PD No. 1445 and Section 9.1.1 of COA Circular 2012-001 dated June 14, 2012 so that propriety and validity of disbursements can be ensured; and



	b)		Require the Accountant and Planning Director to submit the lacking As-built plans of the building projects for COA technical inspection.

		

2)	Disbursement Vouchers for transactions totaling P9.036 million were not submitted to the Auditor’s Office, contrary to the pertinent provisions of Section 7 of COA Circular No. 2009-006 dated September 15, 2009 hence, regularity, validity and occurrence of transactions were not ascertained.



We recommended and the Management agreed to:



	a)	Direct the Accountants to immediately submit the Disbursements Vouchers and supporting documents for the subject transactions totaling P9.036 million in compliance with the pertinent provision of Section 7 of COA Circular 2009-006, dated September 15, 2009, to ascertain regularity, validity and occurrence of the recorded transactions, otherwise the said transactions will be disallowed in audit; and



	b)		Cause the withholding of the salaries of the officials concerned, in case of unjustified failure on their part to submit documents and reports, pursuant to prescribed regulations under Section 7.01 of COA Circular No. 95-006 dated May 18, 1995 and Section 122 of PD No.1445. 



3)	Cash Advances granted to officers and employees totaling P1.584 million as of December 31, 2020 were not liquidated within the prescribed period, contrary to Commission on Audit Circular No. 97-002 dated February 10, 1997, thus, propriety and validity of expenses incurred could not be determined and the related expense accounts were not booked up timely.	

		

We recommended and the Management agreed to:

		

	a)		Strictly comply with the rules and regulations on the grant, utilization and liquidation of cash advances provided under COA Circular No. 97-002 dated February 10, 1997 and direct all concerned AOs the immediate liquidation of the subject cash advances otherwise the Accountant shall cause the withholding of his/her salary as provided under Section 5.1.3 of the COA Circular; and

	b)		Direct the Accountant of Lapaz Campus to refrain from granting additional cash advances to officers and employees with unliquidated cash advances. Further, a certification stating that the employee has no previous unliquidated cash advance must be attached to the disbursement voucher in compliance with COA Circular 2012-001 dated June 14, 2012.



The foregoing findings as well as the other observations and recommendations were discussed with management in an exit conference. Management comments are incorporated in the report where appropriate.
