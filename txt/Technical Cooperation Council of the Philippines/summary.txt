EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

A. Introduction

The Technical Cooperation Council of the Philippines (TCCP) was
created in 1992 by virtue of Executive Order (EO) No. 17 that integrated the
former Technical Committee on Technical Assistance Council (TAC) with the
Inter-Agency Technical Committee on Technical Cooperation among Developing
Countries and Economic Cooperation among Developing Countries (IATC-
TCDC/ECDC).

It serves as the national focal point for Technical Cooperation among
Developing Countries (TCDC) and as such implements a technical cooperation
program in favor of developing countries (DCs) and least developed countries
(LDCs), coordinates related projects and activities implemented by government
and non-government organizations, and undertakes research on the technical
requirements of DCs and LDCs.

TCCP is headed by the Secretary of the Department of Foreign Affairs
(DFA) as Chairman, with the Director-General of the National Economic and
Development Authority (NEDA) as Vice-Chairman and the Secretaries of
Agriculture, Environment and Natural Resources, Science and Technology, and
Trade and Industry as members. The DFA Secretary as Chairman designated
Ambassador Lilybeth R. Deapera as his Chair-Alternate with four regular
employees: Executive Assistant II designated as Administrative Officer (AO) and
Property Officer (PO), Administrative Assistant as Special Disbursing Officer
(SDO), Accountant and Administrative Aide.

D. Scope of Audit

The audit covered the review of accounts and operations of TCCP for CY
2020. It was aimed to: a) ascertain the level of assurance that may be placed on
the Management’s assertion on the financial statements; b) determine the
propriety of transactions as well as extent of compliance on the applicable laws,
rules and regulations; c) recommend agency’s improvement opportunities; and d)



iii

determine the extent of implementation of prior year’s recommendations.
Moreover, the audit was conducted in accordance with International Standards of
Supreme Audit Institutions (ISSAIs).

E. Auditor’s Report on the Financial Statements

The Auditor rendered an unqualified opinion on the fairness of
presentation of the financial statements of TCCP as at December 31, 2020.

F. Summary of Significant Observations and Recommendations

The following are the significant audit observations and corresponding
recommendations, which were discussed with Management officials concerned,
details of which are further discussed in the Part II of this report. Management
views and comments were incorporated where appropriate.

1. Out of the P431,854.06 Information and Communication Technology
Equipment (ICTE), seven items costing P217,091.06 with carrying amount
of P21,709.06 had been unserviceable and were kept in the storage
undisposed for more than five years. (Paragraph 1)

We recommended and the Management agreed to instruct the:

a) Administrative Officer, in his capacity as Property and Supply Officer,
to expedite the completion of the inventory and inspection of all
unserviceable assets, prepare the Inspection Report of Unserviceable
Property (IIRUP), and submit it to the agency’s Disposal Committee
for the immediate disposal of the unserviceable equipment; and

b) Accountant to prepare Journal Entry Voucher (JEV) to record the
impairment loss of the unserviceable properties based on the IIRUP.

2. Acquisition of 50 pieces heritage books reported as inventory items for
distribution totaling P225,000.00 was processed and paid without complete
documentation necessary to determine the validity of the transaction as
required under Section 4(6) of Presidential Decree (PD) No. 1445.
(Paragraph 2)

We recommended and Management agreed to strengthen the agency’s
internal control in the processing and payment of claims/transactions such
that appropriate supporting documents are complete and attached to the DV
upon processing of transaction.



iv

3. Budget allocation of P2,242,000.00 for trainings to implement the Economic
and Technical Skills Program was not utilized as alternative virtual trainings
were not planned at the onset of the Coronavirus Disease (COVID)-19
pandemic. (Paragraph 3)

We recommended and the Management agreed to direct the Administrative
Officer to adapt alternative virtual/online trainings platform to facilitate the
trainings and activities as planned, consistent with its objectives and
mandates under Executive Order (EO) No. 17 s. 1992.

4. Constructive taxes withheld and remittances for payments of expenses and
compensation in CYs 2017 and 2018 disclosed discrepancies of unremitted
amounts totaling P44,286.90, which could not be verified due to incomplete
submission or non-availability of supporting documents for remittances
made. Moreover, TCCP did not withhold taxes on Government Monetary
Payments (GMP) on its acquisition of the coffee table book by Celestino
Palma III, entitled “The Life and Works of Luis Ac-AC of Paete costing
P225.000.00, contrary to Section 4.114-2 of Revenue Regulation No. 16-
2005, as amended, and Section 4.2 of Department of Finance (DOF)-
Department of Budget and Management (DBM)-COA Joint Circular (JC)
No. 1-2000 precluding its remittance to Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR).
(Paragraph 7)

We recommended and the Management agreed to instruct the Accountant to:

a) continue to verify prior years transactions pertaining to the Due to BIR
account to validate its balance, effect the necessary adjustment, and
remit to the BIR whatever remaining balance found; and

b) conform to the responsibilities of an agency as a withholding agent, in
compliance with Section 4.114-2 of RR No. 16-2005, as amended, and
Section 4.2 of DOF-DBM-COA JC No. 1-2000.

G. Implementation of Prior Year’s Audit Recommendations

Of the 10 audit recommendations embodied in the CY 2019 Annual
Audit Report (AAR), eight were implemented while two were not implemented,
details of which are discussed in Part III of this report.
